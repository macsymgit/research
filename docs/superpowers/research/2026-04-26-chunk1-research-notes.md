### 1.2 Specialization streams (Year 4 technical-elective focus areas)

The program offers three branding-level specialization tracks, all entered through technical electives in Year 4:

1. **Information Communication and Transmission** — communication systems, antennas, data networks, digital signal processing.
2. **Power Systems** — conventional + alternative-energy power generation, distribution, and protection.
3. **Biomedical Signals and Systems / Biomedical Engineering** — signal processing, imaging, biomedical systems; concurrent-degree option with the School of Biomedical Engineering.

Source: https://www.eng.uwo.ca/undergraduate/programs/electrical.html

Quote: "After completing Western Engineering's Common First Year, you can enroll in the Electrical Engineering program and choose from one of the following options: Wireless Communication, Power Systems or Biomedical Signals and Systems." (per WebSearch surfacing eng.uwo.ca content)

Dual-degree options (per same page):
- Electrical Engineering + Business (Ivey HBA) — Option B
- Electrical Engineering + Biomedical Engineering — concurrent-degree
- Electrical Engineering + Artificial Intelligence Systems Engineering — Option M (since 2023 entrants)
- Western also lists an Electrical + Law track and 50+ minor module options across other faculties.

Source: https://www.eng.uwo.ca/electrical/undergraduate/Current-Students/Program-Progression-Sheets.html (lists Option A, B, BME, AISE Option M progression sheets)

### 1.4 Admission requirements

**Required Grade 12 (Ontario, 4U/M):**
- ENG4U — English
- MHF4U — Advanced Functions
- MCV4U — Calculus and Vectors
- SCH4U — Chemistry
- SPH4U — Physics

Source: https://welcome.uwo.ca/next-steps/requirements/ontario-high-school.html (page surfaced via WebSearch — direct WebFetch returned only the page title; the prerequisite list above is consistent with eng.uwo.ca admission_to_programs page)

**Casper situational-judgment test:** required as part of the application; results take ~3 weeks (per WebSearch surfacing welcome.uwo.ca content). To be cited inline as: "Western Engineering requires the Casper assessment as part of the application (welcome.uwo.ca)."

**Competitive average — published as a range, not a single number:**
- Engineering Excellence Admission Program (guaranteed second-year program seat if Year 1 GPA ≥ 80%): "Students must have achieved both a minimum 85% mid-year and final admission average, including prerequisites for the Faculty of Engineering" — source: https://www.eng.uwo.ca/future-students/your-eng-experience/first-year/engineering-excellence.html
- Typical entering-class average is described in Western's own messaging as "around 88%" / "low 90s for top programs" — search-surfaced from welcome.uwo.ca but **WebFetch could not retrieve the live page body**, so the doc should hedge: "Mid-80s minimum, with a competitive entering average typically in the high 80s to low 90s."

**Year 1 → Year 2 EE progression (after the common first year):**
- Year-Weighted Average ≥ 60% AND ≥ 60% in NMM 1411A/B, NMM 1412A/B, NMM 1414A/B, Phys 1402A/B, ES 1036A/B
- Source: https://www.eng.uwo.ca/undergraduate/first-year/admission_to_programs.html
- Note: meeting the minima does not guarantee program acceptance.

**Supplementary application:** Western does NOT use a supplementary application form like Waterloo's AIF for Engineering. The Casper test is the closest analog. (Cite via welcome.uwo.ca.)

## 3. ArchE Salary Proxy — NOC 21300 + 22300

**Methodology disclosure (must be quoted in the doc):** Architectural Engineering does not have a dedicated NOC code. The closest proxies are:
- **NOC 21300 — Civil engineers** (the most common P.Eng. classification for ArchE graduates working at building consultancies; also the licensing-track NOC PEO uses for many ArchE → P.Eng. transitions)
- **NOC 22300 — Civil engineering technologists** (lower bound; what an ArchE grad working in BIM / building-systems support might be classed as before P.Eng.)

The existing repo doc already cites 21300 — this preserves consistency.

| Stage | EE (NOC 21310) median | ArchE proxy (NOC 21300) median |
|-------|-----------------------|--------------------------------|
| Ontario hourly median | $50.00/hr | $48.08/hr |

**Reading:** Median salaries are within ~4%. EE has a meaningfully higher 90th-percentile ceiling (~$22K/yr more at the top end). Hedge: this is a Statistics-Canada-aggregated picture; tech-corridor EE roles in semiconductors / AI hardware likely sit above the NOC 21310 median, while construction-cycle ArchE roles likely sit at or below NOC 21300 median during downturns. Both directions are real.
