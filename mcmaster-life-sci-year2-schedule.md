**Prepared:** 2026-06-24
**Purpose:** Personal Year 2 (Level II) course plan for **Honours Life Sciences — Co-op**, premed-focused, with rationale, co-op timeline, and a verification checklist.

**Total units:** 30 (standard full load = 5 + 5)

> **MATH 1LT3 — skipped (deliberate).** Honours Life Sciences flagship does not require the calculus pair, STATS 2B03 does not list it as a prerequisite, and neither the MCAT nor Ontario med schools require Calculus II. The slot is better spent on an MCAT prereq. Only revisit if you later switch into a math-heavy Level II stream or a grad program that demands it.

## Why This Works — Quick Rationale

**1. Honours Life Sci core + premed prereqs, balanced.**
Honours Life Sciences (flagship) has relatively few hard-required courses and a large elective space. This plan completes the LIFESCI/SCICOMM/STATS/Genetics core *and* uses the elective space for the MCAT/med prerequisites premed students must clear — Organic Chemistry I/II, Biochemistry, and Physiology.

**2. GPA risk is balanced across both terms (same logic as Year 1).**
Each term carries exactly **two hard courses** (Fall: CHEM 2OA3 + BIOCHEM 2BB3; Winter: CHEM 2OB3 + BIO 2A03) offset by lighter LIFESCI/SCICOMM/Nutrition courses. You never stack all the weed-outs in one term.

**3. MCAT foundation is laid in the right year.**
Year 2 is where the MCAT science prereqs live: Orgo (CP/BB), Biochem (BB), Physiology (BB), Genetics (BB), Stats (research/CARS-adjacent reasoning). By April 2028 you'll have the content base to take the **FL1 diagnostic** on schedule (per the med-school plan).

**4. Year 2 is the slip year — this schedule protects against it.**
Per `mcmaster-life-sci-to-med-school-plan.md` §6, many premed students drop from 3.90 → 3.78 in Year 2 because orgo + biochem + physiology hit at once. Spreading the two orgo courses across two terms and pairing each with a lighter course is the structural defense.

## Co-op Overlay (Honours Life Sciences — Co-op)

| When | Action |
|---|---|
| **Year 2, Fall–Winter** | You are admitted to / confirming the Co-op stream. Complete the **co-op professional-development / readiness component** (verify exact course code on MAP — often a 0-unit or co-op-prep module). Build résumé + start applying to co-op postings through the co-op portal. |
| **Summer 2028 (May–Aug)** | **First co-op work term** — typically clinical research, pharma, public health, or a hospital research institute. All premed-relevant. ~4 months. |
| **Year 3+** | Additional/longer work term(s); co-op typically **extends the degree toward 5 years**. A later 8–12 month placement is common. Aligns well with the **gap-year MCAT plan** (recommended in the med-school doc). |

**Premed note:** Co-op work terms double as ABS/research entries and can fund MCAT prep — but do **not** let a work-term search pull your GPA down in Year 2. Academics first; co-op applications are a few hours/week.

## Year 2 Action Checklist (in order)

### Before enrollment opens (summer 2027)
- [ ] **Confirm the exact Honours Life Sciences Level II requirements on MAP** (`mapsci.ca/level-2-programs/list/honours-life-sciences/`). Verify which LIFESCI courses are *required* vs *choose-from* — the named set includes LIFESCI 2A03, 2L03, 2CC3, 2D03, 2N03, SCICOMM 2A03, STATS 2B03, BIOLOGY 2B03/2C03. Adjust the two "core" picks here to match the current required list.
- [ ] **Confirm the Co-op prep course code and deadlines** with the Science Co-op office.
- [ ] **Verify prerequisites in Mosaic:** CHEM 2OA3 (needs CHEM 1AA3 ✅), BIOCHEM 2BB3 (needs BIOLOGY 1A03 + CHEM 1AA3 ✅), BIOLOGY 2A03/2C03, STATS 2B03 (Level II Science standing ✅).
- [ ] **Read current Simple Syllabus pages** for CHEM 2OA3/2OB3 and BIOCHEM 2BB3 — instructor and grading scheme change yearly; these are the GPA-critical courses.
- [ ] **Sanity-check instructors on Rate My Courses** (`ratemycourses.io/mcmaster`).

### When enrollment opens
- [ ] Lock **CHEM 2OA3 / 2OB3** first — orgo sections fill fast and are hard to rearrange.
- [ ] Then **BIOCHEM 2BB3**, **STATS 2B03**, **BIOLOGY 2C03**.
- [ ] Then the LIFESCI core + lighter courses.
- [ ] **Re-check for time conflicts** after each add (orgo labs are long blocks).

### During the year
- [ ] **GPA target: maintain 3.90+ cGPA.** This is the Year 2 line that keeps UofT/Mac med in reach.
- [ ] Office hours for CHEM 2OA3 in the first 4 weeks — orgo is the Year 2 weed-out.
- [ ] **Continue your research lab** from summer 2027 (6–8 hrs/wk).
- [ ] **Late March 2028: take the AAMC FL1 MCAT diagnostic** (calibration only) per the med-school plan.
- [ ] Apply for **summer 2028 research/co-op** positions by mid-February 2028.

## What NOT to Worry About in Year 2

- **MATH 1LT3** — not required for flagship Honours Life Sci; skip unless a specific later program demands it.
- **MCAT prep intensity** — Year 2 is content *coverage* (via courses) + one diagnostic, not full prep. Real prep is Year 3 summer onward (gap-year plan).
- **Specialization-specific courses** — flagship Honours Life Sci stays flexible; you don't need to commit to OD/SMS-style course tracks.
- **Co-op vs GPA tension** — keep co-op applications light; a strong GPA beats an early work term for med admissions.
